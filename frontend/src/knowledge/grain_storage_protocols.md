# Kido Protocol: Post-Harvest Grain Storage (Aflatoxin Prevention)
## Overview
Preserving the quality of grains (Maize, Rice, Beans) post-harvest is critical for market value and food safety.

## Dryness Standards
- Grains must be dried to a moisture content (MC) of below 13% before storage.
- Use the **"Glass Jar Test"**: Place a handful of grain in a dry jar with salt. Shake. If the salt sticks to the grain after 10 mins, the MC is too high.

## Storage Techniques
### 1. Hermetic Bags (PICS)
- Use triple-layered hermetic bags. These bags deplete oxygen and increase CO2, naturally suffocating insects without chemicals.
- Do not reuse bags if they have punctures.

### 2. Metal Silos
- For large-scale storage, Kido-certified metal silos must be kept in shaded, well-ventilated areas to prevent condensation.

## Aflatoxin Management
- Avoid harvesting during rainfall.
- Remove broken or discolored grains before storage, as these are primary sites for Aspergillus mold growth.
- Store bags on wooden pallets, never directly on concrete floors.
