# Kido Protocol: High-Value Export Standards (Cocoa & Cashew)
## Overview
To achieve Kido Premium status for international markets (EU/USA), crops must meet strict traceability and quality benchmarks.

## Cocoa Export Grade A
### 1. Fermentation (6-Day Protocol)
- Beans must be fermented in cedar boxes and covered with banana leaves.
- Turn the beans on Day 3 and Day 5 to ensure consistent flavor development.
- Target: Correct "chocolate brown" internal color with a crisp shell.

### 2. Drying & Humidity
- Sun-dry on raised bamboo mats. 
- Never dry cocoa on the ground to avoid contamination.
- Maximum allowable moisture: 7.5%.

## Cashew RCN (Raw Cashew Nut) Standards
- **Outturn Rate (KOR)**: Kido Premium requires a KOR of 48 lbs or higher.
- **Nut Count**: Nuts should be consistent in size, with a count between 180-200 per kg.
- **Defects**: Less than 10% spotted or immature nuts tolerated.

## Traceability DNA
- Every export batch must have its **Heritage Passport** QR code scanned at the farm gate.
- Maintain GPS coordinates of the specific farm plot for EU deforestation compliance (EUDR).
